## Overview

*Mountains Must Have Stones* was my first curatorial project after graduation. The exhibition focused on Chinese landscape art and Eastern philosophy, exploring a quieter and more reflective mode of artistic expression through the relationship between mountains, stones, tea, and the inner self.

<div className="zh">
《山必有石》是我毕业后的第一个策展项目。展览围绕中国山水艺术与东方哲学展开，通过山、石、茶与内在自我的关系，探索一种更安静、内敛且具有反思性的艺术表达方式。
</div>

The title refers to the idea that stones are hidden within mountains, forming their inner structure and strength. In the exhibition, stones became a metaphor for persistence, self-discovery, and the gradual revealing of one’s true essence through artistic practice and reflection.

<div className="zh">
“山必有石”这一主题指向石头隐藏于山体之中，并构成山的内在结构与力量。在展览中，石头也成为一种象征，代表坚持、自我探索，以及人在艺术实践与反思中逐渐显现出的真实本质。
</div>

## Process

![Poster design process](/images/projects/project-03-mountains-must-have-stones-curation/01-process.png)

The project began through the recommendation of Professor Chen from China Foreign Languages University, which gave me the opportunity to collaborate with artist Zhou Jialin. During the preparation stage, I worked closely with the artist, organizers, and collaborators to understand the conceptual direction of the exhibition and translate it into a clear curatorial and visual framework.

<div className="zh">
这个项目起始于中国外国语大学陈老师的推荐，也让我有机会与艺术家周佳林合作。在前期筹备阶段，我与艺术家、主办方和合作团队密切沟通，梳理展览的概念方向，并将其转化为更清晰的策展与视觉框架。
</div>

![Curatorial material process](/images/projects/project-03-mountains-must-have-stones-curation/02-process.jpg)

I was responsible for the overall poster design, team coordination, and exhibition communication. The visual and written materials needed to reflect the calmness of Chinese landscape painting while also communicating the philosophical meaning behind the exhibition title.

<div className="zh">
我主要负责整体海报设计、团队协调与展览沟通。视觉和文字材料需要呈现中国山水绘画中的静谧气质，同时也要传达“山必有石”这一展览主题背后的哲学含义。
</div>

![Exhibition documentation process](/images/projects/project-03-mountains-must-have-stones-curation/03-process.jpg)

In addition to the visual design, I also helped manage promotional content before and during the exhibition. This included organizing information, preparing materials, and coordinating communication between different people involved in the project.

<div className="zh">
除了视觉设计之外，我也参与了展前与展中的宣传内容管理，包括整理展览信息、准备宣传材料，以及协调项目中不同参与者之间的沟通。
</div>

![Exhibition space process](/images/projects/project-03-mountains-must-have-stones-curation/04-process.png)

The exhibition space was arranged to create a quiet and contemplative viewing experience. Through the placement of artworks, printed materials, and spatial rhythm, the project invited viewers to slow down and engage with the symbolic relationship between landscape, stone, and inner reflection.

<div className="zh">
展览空间的布置希望营造一种安静、适合观看与思考的氛围。通过作品、印刷材料与空间节奏的安排，项目邀请观众放慢速度，进入山水、石头与内在观照之间的象征关系。
</div>

## Outcome

![Final exhibition view](/images/projects/project-03-mountains-must-have-stones-curation/05-final.jpg)

The exhibition was completed successfully despite unexpected challenges, including printing limitations and time constraints during the production process. These imperfections became an important part of the learning experience, helping me better understand the practical demands of exhibition planning and execution.

<div className="zh">
尽管在制作过程中遇到了印刷技术和时间限制等突发问题，展览最终仍然顺利完成。这些不完美也成为了很重要的学习经验，让我更真实地理解了展览策划与落地执行中的实际要求。
</div>

Through this project, I gained hands-on experience in curatorial communication, visual production, team coordination, and working under pressure. It also helped me develop a deeper interest in how exhibition-making can connect visual language, cultural memory, and philosophical ideas.

<div className="zh">
通过这个项目，我获得了关于策展沟通、视觉制作、团队协调以及压力下工作的实践经验。它也让我更深入地意识到，展览可以如何连接视觉语言、文化记忆与哲学思考。
</div>

For me, *Mountains Must Have Stones* was not only a curatorial project, but also an early professional experience that shaped how I think about exhibition space, artistic narrative, and the quiet emotional power of cultural symbols.

<div className="zh">
对我来说，《山必有石》不仅是一次策展项目，也是一次重要的早期职业实践。它影响了我后来对于展览空间、艺术叙事，以及文化符号中安静情绪力量的理解。
</div>
