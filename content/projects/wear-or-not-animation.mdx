## Overview

*Wear or Not* is an experimental hand-drawn animation about clothing, body image, and the invisible social rules placed on women. The project reflects on how an everyday decision — what to wear — can become connected to judgment, shame, protection, and self-expression.

<div className="zh">
《Wear or Not》是一部关于穿着、身体形象与女性所承受的隐性社会规则的手绘实验动画。项目思考一个日常选择——“穿什么”——如何与评判、羞耻、保护和自我表达联系在一起。
</div>

The animation uses a soft hand-drawn style to approach a sensitive subject in a personal and poetic way. Instead of giving a direct answer, the work stays inside the uncertainty between wanting to be seen and wanting to stay safe.

<div className="zh">
动画使用柔和的手绘风格，以更个人和诗性的方式处理这个敏感主题。作品并不试图给出直接答案，而是停留在“想被看见”和“想要安全”之间的矛盾中。
</div>

## Process

![Early sketches and visual ideas](/images/projects/project-02-wear-or-not-animation/01-process.png)

The project began with sketches of clothing, bodies, and repeated emotional states. I used simple lines and pink tones to create a visual world that feels soft on the surface, while still carrying tension underneath.

<div className="zh">
项目最初从关于衣服、身体和重复情绪状态的草图开始。我使用简单线条和粉色调建立一个表面柔软、但内部带有紧张感的视觉世界。
</div>

![Script and narrative notes](/images/projects/project-02-wear-or-not-animation/02-process.png)

The narrative was developed through written notes and scene planning. I focused on small everyday gestures and internal monologues rather than a linear plot, showing how social pressure can quietly enter the body.

<div className="zh">
叙事通过文字笔记和场景规划逐渐形成。我没有使用完整线性情节，而是关注日常动作和内心独白，表现社会压力如何悄悄进入身体。
</div>

![Editing process](/images/projects/project-02-wear-or-not-animation/03-process.png)

During editing, I arranged the drawings into a rhythmic sequence. The pacing was designed to feel repetitive and slightly uncomfortable, echoing the cycle of looking, judging, hiding, and choosing again.

<div className="zh">
在剪辑过程中，我将手绘画面整理成带有节奏的影像序列。整体节奏带有重复和轻微不适感，呼应观看、评判、隐藏和再次选择之间的循环。
</div>

## Outcome

![Final animation frames](/images/projects/project-02-wear-or-not-animation/04-final.png)

The final animation became a quiet reflection on how clothing can function as both expression and protection. Through hand-drawn images, fragmented scenes, and a personal visual language, the project questions how much freedom we really have when presenting our bodies in public.

<div className="zh">
最终动画成为一次关于衣服如何同时作为表达与保护的安静反思。通过手绘画面、碎片化场景和个人化的视觉语言，作品追问当我们在公共空间中呈现身体时，究竟拥有多少真正的自由。
</div>

This project was one of my early explorations of feminist visual storytelling. It helped me understand how animation can transform private discomfort into a visual form that feels gentle, vulnerable, and critical at the same time.

<div className="zh">
这个项目也是我早期对于女性主义视觉叙事的探索之一。它让我理解到，动画可以如何将私人化的不适转化为一种同时柔软、脆弱又具有批判性的视觉形式。
</div>
